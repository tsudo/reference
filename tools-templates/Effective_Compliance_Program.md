Original format: `docx`

Effective Compliance Program (according to HHC and OIG)

1. Implement compliance and practice standards; policies, procedures, and standards of conduct
2. Designate a compliance officer or contact
3. Conduct appropriate training and education
4. Develop open lines of communication
5. Conduct internal monitoring and auditing
6. Enforce disciplinary standards through well-publicized guidelines
7. Respond appropriately to detected offenses and develop and implement corrective action

### Implement Compliance and Practice Standards

* Create HIPAA Privacy, Security, and Medical Billing policies, procedures, and standards of conduct
* Review through monitoring/auditing on an annual basis
* Make available to all staff
* Acknowledged by all staff

### Designate a compliance officer or contact

* Compliance Officer – responsible for coordination and completion
  + Security
  + Privacy
  + Medical Billing
* Create a Compliance Committee
  + Routine meetings

### Conduct Appropriate Training and Education

* Determine who needs training
* Determine type of training
* Determine when and how often
* Document training

### Develop Open Lines of Communication

* Making updates to policy/processes available
* User friendly process for anonymous reporting of potential misconduct
* User friendly access to policies and procedures

### Conduct Internal Auditing and Monitoring

* Formal review of compliance with particular set of standards
  + Identifies risk areas
  + What are we monitoring and auditing?
  + EX: EMR logins, AD logins, policies, coding and billing
* Set a specific schedule

### Enforce Disciplinary Standards Through Well Publicized Guidelines

* Clearly written policies outlining actions for non-compliance
* Define non-compliance
* Communicated through training and examples

### Respond to Detected Offenses and Develop Corrective Action Initiatives

* Defined process for responding to detected violation
* Compliance plan should include monitoring and warning indicators
* Defined actions for analysis
* Defined issues requiring corrective action plan

3 Levels

List out typical attributes in each level

Compliance Focus…

* Have policies but are not reviewed regularly
* Are not documented in a manner that allows for ease of access by staff
* No formal compliance committee that reviews and monitors
* Training is typically only upon hire and annually
* Communication of policy occurs to users, no single source of access

Promote awareness and change …

* Policies are well known and reviewed continuously by compliance committee
* Internal monitoring and auditing occurs, but not in a defined routine timeline
* Repository of policy and communication to all users – ease of access for acknowledgement of policies

Long-Term Sustainment … Risk management focus

* Compliance committee maintains governance of policies, routinely reviewed, and updated, risks are identified and analyzed, tracked, and mitigated
* Implementation of well documented monitoring and auditing on a defined routine basis
